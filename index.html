My name is Joaquin Flores-Cadena. I was born on February 21, 2002. I am Mexican American male (he/him), and I have one older sister name Jessica Flores.

I was born in Huntington Park, California. I grew up in the same city my whole childhood. I grew up with my parents and my sister for my early years of life, until we moved in with my grandma the summer going into 3rd grade. This is where I live currently. Growing up I was faced with the challenge of needing to adapt quickly. I was moving across town, to a new school, moving in with my grandma who only spoke Spanish. So I needed to not only make new friends, but needed to learn a whole new language.

I would attend three different elementary schools, State St Elementary (Pre-k - 2nd), Miles Elementary (3rd-4th), and San Antonio Elementary School (5th). I was forced to move schools because of location, so I was had to be social to make friends. Luckily I attended Gage Middle School (6th-8th) in the magnet program, which had most of my friends from all three elementaries, so I had friends from everywhere. I attended Bravo Medical Magnet High School (9th-12th), a school that was located in Los Angeles 30 minutes from my home in Huntington Park. In High School I had some friends from Middle School who attended Bravo, but I once again needed to make new friends. I was able to make new friends, and join the medical pathway program in High School, along with making the Varsity baseball team the first year it was created my senior year. Now I attend UCR where I am a 4th year biology major, with hopes of workin somwhere in the medical field.
